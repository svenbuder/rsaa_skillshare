# RSAA Skillshare 2026-02 Refining Research Questions Worksheet

Use this worksheet as a tool to practise turning scientific interest into a usable research question for Honours/PhD projects, paper writing, and postdoc idea generation.
\vspace{-0.1cm}

## Conceptual question (what you want to understand)

Write the question you *actually* care about. “How”, “why”, or “how much” questions are encouraged here. *Try to phrase this so you could imagine answering part of it within a PhD, not a career.*

\vspace{0.6cm}  

## Testable reformulation (force clarity)

Rewrite your question so it could plausibly be answered with “Yes”, “No”, or “It depends (and here is why)”. Some ideas: (i) Is X larger/smaller than Y under condition Z? (ii) Does method A outperform method B in regime R? (iii) Is effect E present above/below threshold T?
**Testable version:**  
\vspace{0.6cm}  

**I would consider this question answered if:**  
\vspace{0.6cm}  

## Refined research question (return with insight)

Using the testable version above, rewrite your question as a **sharper conceptual question** that could plausibly be answered within a few months of focused work. 
This should now be more specific about (i) what is being compared, (ii) under which conditions, and (iii) why it is interesting. **Refined research question:**

## First project step (reality check)

A useful research question should suggest a concrete next action. **If you worked on this for the next 4–8 weeks, what would you do first?** (e.g. data, method, comparison, test)
